Historical Background of Boujaad Rugs

Boujaad rugs have their origins in the Middle Atlas region of Morocco, where various tribes, including the Glaoui, have played a crucial role in their development. These vibrant rugs often reflect the cultural narratives and traditions of the Berber people who inhabit that area. The mountainous landscape influences the artistic expressions found in these textiles, showcasing the rich heritage of the tribes. This regional craftsmanship sets them apart from other Moroccan rugs, highlighting the difference between Beni Ourain and Boujaad in both style and meaning.

Crafted primarily in small villages, Boujaad rugs feature distinct patterns and colors that tell stories of the weavers’ lives and experiences. The designs often incorporate bold geometric shapes, contrasting with the more subdued tones of Beni Ourain rugs. This visual differentiation emphasizes the difference between Beni Ourain and Boujaad, as each rug carries unique cultural significance rooted in local traditions and environmental influences.
